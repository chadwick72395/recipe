Poor Man’s Burnt Ends

Estimated Time to Completion: 4.5–5 hours

Equipment

  • Smoker
  • Aluminum foil
  • Knife
  • Cutting board
  • Aluminum pan or foil tray
  • Tongs

Ingredients

  • 1 chuck roast (2–3 lbs)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2–3 tbsp butter
  • 2–3 tbsp brown sugar
  • ¼ cup BBQ sauce

How To

  1. Preheat smoker to 225°F.
  2. Prep roast: Pat the chuck roast dry and lightly coat with olive oil.
  3. Season: Apply salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika evenly.
  4. Smoke: Place roast directly on the smoker grates and cook about 3 hours, until internal temperature reaches around 160°F.
  5. Cube the meat: Remove roast and cut into 1–1.5 inch cubes.
  6. Add to pan: Place cubes into an aluminum pan and add butter, brown sugar, and BBQ sauce.
  7. Cover and return to smoker for 1.5–2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  8. Finish: Cook until the cubes become tender and caramelized.
  9. They should be soft, smoky, and slightly sticky when done.
