Smoked Baby Back Ribs

Estimated Time to Completion: 5–6 hours

Equipment

  • Smoker (pellet smoker recommended)
  • Meat thermometer
  • Aluminum foil
  • Tongs
  • Spray bottle (optional)

Ingredients

  • 2 racks baby back pork ribs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 2–3 tbsp apple juice per rack (for wrap)
  • BBQ sauce (optional)

How To

  1. Preheat smoker to 225°F.
  2. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs and pat dry.
  3. Lightly coat ribs with olive oil.
  4. Season both sides evenly with salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and brown sugar.
  5. Place ribs meat side up on the smoker.
  6. Smoke for 3 hours at 225°F. Optional: lightly spray with apple juice every 45–60 minutes.
  7. Remove ribs and place meat side down on aluminum foil.
  8. Add 2–3 tbsp apple juice to the foil and wrap tightly.
  9. Return to smoker and cook another 1.5–2 hours.
  10. Unwrap ribs and place back on smoker meat side up.
  11. Cook 30–45 minutes to firm the bark. Optional: brush BBQ sauce during this stage.
  12. Ribs are done around 195–203°F internal and when the bones begin to pull back.
  13. Rest 10 minutes, slice, and serve.
