Garlic Butter Thighs (Smoker)

Estimated Time to Completion: 25–30 minutes

Equipment

  • Smoker
  • Cast iron skillet
  • Meat thermometer
  • Tongs
  • Small bowl

Ingredients

  • 6–8 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted
  • 4–5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp onion powder (optional)

How To

  1. Preheat smoker to 350–375°F and place a cast iron skillet inside while it heats.
  2. Pat chicken thighs dry and lightly coat with olive oil.
  3. Season both sides with salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, and onion powder.
  4. In a small bowl mix melted butter and minced garlic.
  5. Place chicken thighs into the hot cast iron skillet and cook 10–12 minutes.
  6. Flip the chicken and pour the butter garlic mixture into the skillet.
  7. Spoon the butter over the chicken.
  8. Cook another 10–15 minutes until internal temperature reaches 175°F.
  9. Spoon remaining garlic butter from the skillet over the chicken before serving.
