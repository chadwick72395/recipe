Garlic Butter Smoked Chicken Thighs

Estimated Time to Completion: 1.5–2 hours

Equipment

  • Smoker (pellet smoker recommended)
  • Meat thermometer
  • Tongs
  • Cast iron skillet
  • Small saucepan

Ingredients

  • 6–8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp parsley (optional)

How To

  1. Preheat smoker to 225°F.
  2. Pat chicken thighs dry and rub lightly with olive oil.
  3. Season evenly with salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika.
  4. Place thighs skin side up directly on smoker grates.
  5. Smoke 60–75 minutes until internal temperature reaches 155–160°F.
  6. In a small saucepan melt butter and add minced garlic. Cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  7. Place thighs skin side up in a cast iron skillet.
  8. Pour the garlic butter into the pan around the chicken.
  9. Return skillet to smoker and cook until chicken reaches 175°F internal temperature.
  10. Spoon some butter from the pan over the chicken once or twice while finishing.
  11. Optional: sprinkle with parsley before serving.
